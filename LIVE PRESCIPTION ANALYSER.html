How OCR works · tips for best results
Pipeline: Each photo is preprocessed (grayscale, contrast stretching, Otsu adaptive thresholding, upscaled to 1800–2400 px wide) then read by Tesseract.js with multiple page-segmentation modes: uniform-block, sparse-text (catches scattered drug names that single-block mode misses), and one pass on the raw image as fallback. Lines from all passes are merged and deduplicated to capture every drug.

Tips:
  • Good even lighting (no shadow on the prescription)
  • Hold camera steady — let autofocus lock before tapping shutter
  • Fill the frame — get close to the writing
  • If a drug is mis-read, edit the text in box 2 and tap Analyze
  • Fuzzy matching: minor typos (e.g. "Clarithromicin" → Clarithromycin) are tolerated automatically via Levenshtein distance
  • First OCR run downloads ~2 MB English language data (cached afterwards; works offline after first use)

How to refresh this reference

  1. On the review date, scan the sources above for new label changes, guideline updates, or major trials.
  2. Open this HTML file in a text editor.
  3. Update the JS data blocks: cvDrugs, icuDrugs, rules, condRules, icuCondRules, conditions.
  4. Update the “Compiled on” date at the top of the script. (const COMPILED_DATE = '...')
  5. Save and reopen the file in your browser.
  6. Optionally re-run the spot-check tests (see the README/comments in the file).
Tip: Subscribe to FDA Drug Safety alerts, ESC/ACC press releases, and IDSA guideline updates so refreshes are evidence-driven, not calendar-driven alone.

Method & scope

Interactions are encoded as drug- or class-level rules. Resolution priority: drug×drug > drug×class > class×drug > class×class > default (none documented). Mechanisms covered: CYP450 isoenzyme inhibition/induction (3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, 1A2), P-glycoprotein, OATP1B1, pharmacodynamic additivity (QT, bleeding, BP, K+), gastrointestinal pH and chelation absorption effects, and gut-flora-mediated effects.

Comorbidity dosing covers: CKD by CrCl band (3/4/5+HD), hepatic impairment by Child-Pugh class, pregnancy by trimester & lactation, COPD/asthma, hematologic conditions (bleeding diathesis, thrombocytopenia, anemia), seizure disorder/AED interactions, HFrEF, AV block/bradycardia, hypotension, hyperkalemia tendency, and elderly/frailty (age ≥75). Dose recommendations refer to US labeling unless noted; European and society-specific guidelines may differ.
